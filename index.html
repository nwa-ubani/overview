Case Study 2: Strategic Card Adoption Revolution

Strategic Card Adoption Revolution

The Challenge: Despite having a robust digital platform, physical card adoption was dismally low. Customers not using cards. Repeat usage was moderate.

The Findings:

Rather than launching generic card promotions, I did a little research on customers who hadn't used their cards. Customers fell into distinct behavioural segments that required completely different approaches.

- Some had cards but hadn't activated them.
- Some requested cards but never received them.
- While others who needed cards weren't even asking.

The Segmentation Breakthrough:

  • The Never-Askers: Identified 23,000+ users who'd never requested cards despite having payment history indicating 'high card usage potential'
  • The Lost Requesters: Found 8,400 customers who'd requested cards 30+ days ago but never received them—immediate escalation to cards team
  • The Payment Validators: Used transaction history to identify customers who regularly made payments, indicating financial engagement and card readiness
  • Cross-Team Coordination: Established direct communication protocols with the cards team to resolve fulfillment bottlenecks in real-time

The Multi-Channel Execution:

I created targeted campaigns for each segment. 'What is in it for them' content like cross-border payments, card control to avoid auto debit or to avoid card declining fee, for never-askers.

Escalation protocols for lost requesters, and premium card benefits messaging for high-value customers. The result was a 73% increase in meaningful card engagement.

Case Study 3: Driving Transaction on Dormant Accounts

Driving Transaction on Dormant Accounts

The Problem: Thousands of customers had funded their accounts but were sitting idle. Money was there, intent was unclear, and the business was missing massive transaction volume opportunities. These "funded but not transacting" accounts represented untapped potential worth millions. While at a potential risk to churn.

Segmentation and Behavioural Approach:

Instead of generic "please transact" messages, I looked deeper to spot opportunity gaps and/or loopholes. For low hanging fruits, I segmented into 2 segments, and pushed it with an ongoing weekly promo that made customers who had ₦5,000 in their accounts and performed a transaction, eligible for a raffle draw to win monetary and non-monetary rewards.

- Segment 1 were customers with ₦5,000.
- Segment 2 were customers with less than ₦5,000.

This insight became the foundation for a revolutionary segmentation strategy.

The Strategy:

  • Account Balance with ₦5,000: Low-risk transaction incentives messaging.
  • Lower-Balance Segment (<₦5,000): "You're ₦1000 away from participating today" messaging with encouragement to reach the ₦5,000 threshold.
  • Time-Sensitive Triggers: Identified optimal engagement windows—customers who hadn't transacted in 14, 30, and 60-day ranges received different urgency levels and messaging
  • Value Demonstration: Created scenario-based content showing exactly how transactions would benefit each segment's specific situation

The Psychological Triggers:

Funded accounts indicated intent but required confidence building. I crafted campaigns that addressed common transaction hesitations while providing clear value propositions tailored to each customer's demonstrated financial behaviour, previous buyer history.

Case Study 4: Membership Platform Resurrection

Membership Platform Resurrection

The Crisis: A membership platform was in decline with reactivation rates plummeting to just 12%, while family plan adoption—critical for long-term value—remained stubbornly low. The business model was fundamentally broken, with acquisition costs far exceeding reactivation investment.

Systematic Revival Strategy:

I approached this like a medical diagnosis, analyzing why members left and what would bring them back. I discovered that generic win-back campaigns were actually driving people further away. The solution required precision in targeting and deeply personalizing value propositions.

The Reactivation Science:

  • Behavioural Archaeology: Analyzed past member activity to identify "reactivation DNA", i.e. specific patterns that predicted successful returns with 84% accuracy
  • Emotional Journey Mapping: Created campaigns addressing why members left (value perception, life changes, competitive alternatives) rather than just promoting features
  • Progressive Value Delivery: Designed comeback experiences that immediately demonstrated enhanced value within first 48 hours of reactivation
  • Family Network Expansion: Built referral systems that turned individual reactivations into household adoptions, multiplying lifetime value

The Family Plan Revolution:

I recognized that family plans weren't selling because they were positioned as "more expensive" rather than "more valuable per person." I restructured messaging around shared experiences, cost-per-member savings, and household health goals, resulting in a 127% conversion increase.

The Compound Effect:

By combining predictive reactivation with family expansion, I created a growth flywheel where every reactivated member became a potential advocate for 2-3 additional family memberships, exponentially increasing customer lifetime value.
